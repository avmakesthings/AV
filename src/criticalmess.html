CRITICAL MESS

Digital Design. Prototyping. Fabrication

A rather messy solution to the Pacific Trash Gyre and displacement of Pacific island communities due to sea level rise.

The Problem

In honor of the voyage of the Plastiki in 2010, Architecture for Humanity(AFH) initiated a multi-national studio that involved schools of architecture around the Pacific Rim, from California to Australia, along the Plastiki Expedition route. The brief, conceptual projects, all connected by the Pacific Ocean, are centered on restoring water quality, protecting inhabitants from climate change, creating sustainable livelihoods and safe housing.

UC Berkeley was the first stop on the Pac Rim Studio journey, with the focus centered on developing temporary or permanent islands to address habitat and housing loss with rising sea levels. We also looked at the Pacific Trash Gyre and started thinking about ways that these two problems could be tackled by one solution.

The Team

I worked on this project with Scott Ewart and we both collaborated over the 3 week duration to develop the concept, build some pretty questionable models and present our ideas graphically. Here we are looking rather young in this charming studio photo.

The Design Process

This was a fast, conceptual project that was really about quickly iterating through ideas and presenting them in a schematic way. The focus was really on the big picture and conveying the concept through compelling imagery. We were interested in constructing islands in the most natural way .. instead of highly planned we wanted to create a system that could grow organically.
