SECRET SCOOP

UX research, UI|UX Design

A website redesign that aimed to both update the functionality of former site for a San Francisco food start-up and provide a more user friendly interface.

The Problem

Founded in 2012, Secret Scoop is a Thai gelato business in San Francisco. Their products are made to order for individuals, catered events and festivals. Although the aesthetics of their product images are quite impressive, there were a number of flaws on their website. In order to stay current, Secret Scoop wanted a redesign of their website to boost profits but still wanted to maintain the aesthetic look of their brand image.

The Users

Users found the old Secret Scoop website difficult to navigate, slow and graphically unclear.

--User experience testing for Secret Scoop

We began the process by interviewing four Secret Scoop Web users in order to identify issues that need to be resolved with the current website. We also recorded their requirements and preferences for future steps of improvement.

The Team

My partner Funn Fisher and I worked on this project as UI/UX designers and web developers. We pair designed and collaborated on each task in order to get the optimal and most efficient outcome for the business.

Design Issues

1| Users get lost in navigation, especially when trying to place an order online.
2| Small scroll bars for descriptions are not wanted.
3| Numerous pages just for photos are not necessary.
4| There are no payment methods to submit an order.
5| Users wanted to easily access contact information.
6| Users wanted updates on where Secret Scoop would be available at future events or festivals.

The Design Process

Initial prototyping

After some preliminary user research we went through an exploratory paper prototyping phase, where we brainstormed different features and interaction flows. Based on user needs, we decided against developing a mobile application, in favor of a single responsive site. We also looked at Secret Scoops existing content and started aggregating content to form a cohesive story about the business.

Interactive Prototyping

Interactive prototypes are one of the most important and exciting parts of the design process, where you see your ideas manifested in a more tangible way. For Secret Scoop, hi-fidelity and interactive prototyping was done with Justinmind and then mocked up with bootstrap. It was important that the content we chose tell the right story and represent the brand in the most compelling and honest way. This involved repeatedly testing the interactive prototypes with different images and text to see what looked the best and most fit with brand image. Responsive design was also critical, so the site was tested both with Chrome’s developer tools and on different devices, to ensure consistency.

Usability Testing

While we tried to the best of our ability to base our redesign of the website on the feedback of our initial user study, it’s hard to evaluate this type of performance without usability testing. We discovered that certain aspects of the site were favored, such as the overall aesthetics, colors, fonts and general layout. It seemed that in general the users needs were met and the client was happy with the improvements, however almost all of our users reported issues with the navigation and interaction design of the online store. Consequently, we’re currently in the process of redesigning this part of the site.

What we learned

Other than learning that Secret Scoop Gelato is unbelievably addictive, one of the main takeaways from this project was that doing work with a client that you like can be incredibly rewarding. We found that maintaining clear and ongoing communication with the Secret Scoop ensured that expectations were met and that the project was delivered in a timely fashion. This project has also helped us streamline our process. For instance, I would probably just use something like Bootstrap to quickly mock up a website, rather than also building an interactive prototype with Justinmind or Axure.

At any rate, it was fun (I believe strongly in working on things you enjoy) and we got some free ice-cream so all’s well…
